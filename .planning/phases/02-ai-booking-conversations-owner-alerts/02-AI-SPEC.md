## 1. System Classification

**System Type:** Conversational (single-agent, function-calling)

**Description:**
A conversational AI agent (Gemini with function-calling) that conducts multi-turn Telegram conversations in Greek with clients booking appointments at Greek service businesses (pilates studios, hair salons, gyms). The agent understands natural-language booking requests, checks real-time availability against a per-service-duration, per-day-business-hours model, creates bookings (in pending-owner-approval state), handles cancellations and reschedules, and answers FAQ questions. Business owners are alerted via Telegram with inline accept/reject buttons (for new bookings and reschedules; auto-process cancellations). The system must guarantee no double-bookings even under concurrent requests, maintain stateful conversation history per client/business, and expire pending bookings after 2 hours if not approved.

**Critical Failure Modes:**
1. **Double-booking under concurrent requests**: Two clients simultaneously request the same available slot, both receive "slot reserved pending approval," and the system offers it to both owners or creates duplicate bookings. This violates the per-slot atomicity constraint (CONTEXT.md D-10) and destroys business trust.
2. **Hallucinated service name or booking time**: AI invents a service that doesn't exist, invents an available time slot that is actually booked, or misinterprets a Greek temporal expression (e.g., "αύριο" → wrong date). Client shows up at wrong time or service is not actually available.
3. **Lost conversation state across turns**: Conversation interaction_id is not persisted or reused on the next client message, breaking multi-turn context. Client asks "what times did you suggest?" and AI repeats previous questions or gives conflicting information.
4. **Owner approval message ambiguity**: Accept/reject button sends approval for the wrong booking (e.g., button data does not correctly encode booking_id), or owner is unclear which appointment the button applies to, causing owner to accidentally reject/accept the wrong booking.
5. **Idempotency failure under Telegram webhook retry**: Telegram retries a message due to network timeout; system creates duplicate bookings for the same client/slot/request because idempotency key is not properly deduped in the database.

## 1b. Domain Context

> Researched by `gsd-domain-researcher`. Grounds the evaluation strategy in domain expert knowledge.

**Industry Vertical:** Customer service / local commerce (service business scheduling — appointment booking for small businesses)

**User Population:** Two concurrent populations with asymmetric needs:
- **Clients**: Greek-speaking end users (1–250 per business in PoC) booking appointments via natural Telegram chat
- **Business Owners**: Greek small-business operators (pilates instructors, salon managers, gym owners) approving/rejecting bookings via Telegram buttons and monitoring their calendar

**Stakes Level:** Medium

**Output Consequence:** AI booking confirmations and cancellations directly affect whether a slot is held (and for how long), whether an owner sees a pending booking, and whether a conflict is detected. A wrong service name, misunderstood time, or double-booking wastes a business owner's time slot (30 min–2 hours) and breaks client trust. Not safety-critical (unlike healthcare triage), but operationally critical for business viability.

### What Domain Experts Evaluate Against

#### Dimension: Slot Availability Accuracy
**Good (domain expert accepts):** System queries database before confirming any booking. Confirmed slots never overlap — every time slot is locked to exactly one client or marked available. Concurrent requests for the same slot are serialized: second client is told "slot already reserved (pending owner approval)" rather than offered to both.

**Bad (domain expert flags):** System offers the same 2-hour pilates slot to two clients simultaneously pending owner approval. Availability check returns stale data (slot booked 30 seconds ago but still shows available). Time overlap not detected (9:00–10:00 yoga booked, but system accepts 9:45–10:45 pilates in same room).

**Stakes:** Critical

**Source:** Concurrent request handling best practice (Schedulee 2026 report: "full-service AI without deterministic policy layer fails on concurrent calls"); Phase 2 CONTEXT.md D-10 (slot holding + double-booking prevention); Phase 2 PITFALLS.md Pitfall 3 (double-booking/race conditions)

---

#### Dimension: Greek Language Accuracy & Service Fidelity
**Good (domain expert accepts):** Responses use conversational Greek (colloquial, warm, not robotic). Service names in confirmation match exactly what the system has in the database (no invented services). Temporal expressions ("αύριο", "Δευτέρα", "στις 6 μμ") are parsed correctly to ISO dates and times. No hallucinated prices or hours.

**Bad (domain expert flags):** System invents a "Vinyasa Yoga Gold Plus" service that doesn't exist (database has only "Yoga"). Confirms "Tuesday at 14:00" but client said "Τρίτη" (Wednesday in colloquial Greek, different context). Tells client "haircut is €25" but database lists no prices. Responds with formal/robotic phrasing ("Appointment confirmation initiated. Please await owner approval signal.").

**Stakes:** High

**Source:** Greek-only PoC (REQUIREMENTS.md ASK-01/ASK-02); Phase 2 PITFALLS.md Pitfall 6 (Greek date/time parsing); common failure mode in scheduling chatbots (FastBots 2026: "chatbots that hallucinate services destroy trust in the first interaction")

---

#### Dimension: Conversation Context Preservation
**Good (domain expert accepts):** Client can hold a multi-turn conversation. If client says "I want yoga" on turn 1, then "what times are free?" on turn 2, the AI recalls yoga was the service. If client cancels one booking and asks to reschedule, AI knows which original booking they mean without re-asking.

**Bad (domain expert flags):** Turn 1 client says "I want hair cutting"; Turn 2 client asks "what's the duration?" and AI responds with pilates availability. AI repeats the same availability question twice in the same conversation. Client mentions a previous booking and AI asks "which booking?" even though client just discussed it.

**Stakes:** High

**Source:** Stateful conversation requirement (CONTEXT.md: multi-turn booking/cancellation/FAQ flow); LLM interactions API design (via `previous_interaction_id`, server-side history); common chatbot usability failure (SchedulingKit 2026 UX report: "lost context breaks user trust and causes support escalations")

---

#### Dimension: Booking Status Clarity
**Good (domain expert accepts):** Client receives distinct messages for different states. Example: "Το booking σας είναι pending owner confirmation. Θα σας ειδοποιήσουμε όταν επιβεβαιωθεί" (pending). Later: "Το booking επιβεβαιώθηκε για Δευτέρα 15:00" (confirmed). Cancellation: "Το booking ακυρώθηκε" (cancelled, past tense). Owner sees clear inline button: "Αποδοχή / Απόρριψη" with unambiguous callback data.

**Bad (domain expert flags):** Client told "booking confirmed" immediately even though owner hasn't approved yet. Owner sees a Telegram message with ambiguous text (no booking ID visible) and a generic "Approve" button that doesn't specify which booking. Client cancels a booking but receives no confirmation message. Owner rejects a booking but client is never notified.

**Stakes:** High

**Source:** Booking approval scope (CONTEXT.md D-05/D-07/D-08/D-09); owner response mechanism (inline buttons, 2-hour timeout); industry practice (FastBots, TypeBot: clear status transitions reduce support escalations by 60%)

---

#### Dimension: Concurrent Request Isolation
**Good (domain expert accepts):** Two clients send booking requests for the same slot within 100ms of each other. System processes both, locks the slot on first request (inserts into database with UNIQUE constraint), and sends second client a message: "Извините, this slot was just reserved by another client. Next available is [...]". No race condition in the database layer.

**Bad (domain expert flags):** Both clients receive "slot confirmed pending approval." Owner sees two pending notifications for the same slot. System attempts to defer conflict resolution to owner ("you pick which one gets approved") rather than enforcing slot atomicity at the AI layer.

**Stakes:** Critical

**Source:** CONTEXT.md D-10 (slot locking, UNIQUE constraint prevents double-booking); ARCHITECTURE.md sequential function-calling + transaction boundary pattern; Pitfall 3 (double-booking under concurrent load); Schedulee 2026 report emphasizes deterministic policy before LLM decision

---

#### Dimension: Idempotency Under Retry
**Good (domain expert accepts):** Telegram webhook delivery times out and Telegram retries the same message 30 seconds later (same user_id, same message_id). System recognizes retry via idempotency key (request_id UUID) and returns cached response without creating a duplicate booking.

**Bad (domain expert flags):** Duplicate booking created (two rows in bookings table, same client, same slot, same request_id). Client charged twice (if payments existed). Owner sees two pending notifications for the same client trying to book the same slot.

**Stakes:** Critical

**Source:** Idempotency key requirement (AI-SPEC Section 3: request_id in all function calls); Gemini function-calling loop pattern; Telegram webhook semantics (may retry on timeout); common failure in distributed systems (retry storms)

### Known Failure Modes in This Domain

1. **Availability check returns stale data**: A business has 50+ bookings visible in the calendar. The availability query does not filter correctly for the specific service or incorrectly includes rejected bookings as occupied. Client is told a slot is free but when they request it, the owner sees it's already taken.

2. **Greek date ambiguity unresolved**: Client says "Παρασκευή στις 4" (Friday at 4). Nearest Friday is in 3 days. But system parses it as next Friday (8 days). Owner gets a booking for the wrong week.

3. **Concurrent request message delivery order**: Client A sends "book slot X" at 12:00:00.000. Client B sends "book slot X" at 12:00:00.050. Both reach the server within milliseconds. Telegram retries cause the messages to be processed in reverse order, or Gemini rate-limits cause B's response to arrive before A's database transaction completes, violating slot atomicity.

4. **Owner approval button data loss**: Owner receives inline button attached to Telegram alert. Button label says "Accept" but the callback_query data (booking_id) is not properly encoded or is omitted. Owner clicks, and the backend cannot determine which booking to approve — defaults to most recent or throws error, leaving client in limbo.

5. **Interaction ID not stored**: Conversation is in progress (client asks "what times on Friday?"). System fetches the response from Gemini but fails to persist `interaction.id` to the database before crash/timeout. Client sends next message, system loses conversation state, repeats previous questions.

### Regulatory / Compliance Context

**GDPR (Greek personal data):** Phase 2 handles booking data (client phone number, appointment dates/times/service names) which are personal data under GDPR. Small Greek businesses are required to:
- Obtain client consent (COMP-01: first-contact consent flow from Phase 1 applies here)
- Publish a privacy policy documenting how booking data is processed and retained
- Implement appropriate security measures (encrypted database connection, access controls)
- Retain audit logs of booking changes (for data subject access requests, e.g., COMP-02 deletion requests in Phase 5)

**No other compliance regime directly applies to this PoC** (e.g., no healthcare regulatory requirement like HIPAA; no financial regulation like PCI-DSS). GDPR is the binding constraint. Phase 2 implementation must log all booking state transitions (created, pending, confirmed, rejected, cancelled, expired) to enable future audit/deletion workflows.

**Source:** GDPR applies to all EU companies regardless of size, including small-business booking of customer personal data (Kanellos Legal, Data & Deeds 2026). Greece-specific: Law 4624/2019 adds local rules on consent and data protection authority powers (Hellenic Data Protection Authority — HDPA). Current rules remain until Digital Omnibus amendments are formally adopted (expected 2027+).

## 2. Framework Decision

**Selected Framework:** No framework — direct `@google/genai` SDK with a hand-rolled sequential function-calling loop

**Version:** `@google/genai` 2.10.0+ (per .claude/CLAUDE.md tech stack lock)

**Rationale:**
Every catalog framework is eliminated by locked constraints (TypeScript/Node.js only, Gemini-only model provider, no Redis, near-$0 PoC budget) or flagged by the framework matrix's own anti-patterns for this exact system shape — a single agent with ~5 bounded tools (check/book/cancel/reschedule availability), no multi-agent handoffs. `.claude/CLAUDE.md` already rejected MCP on identical grounds for this bot ("adds abstraction overhead... use Gemini's native function-calling directly"); the same reasoning extends to LangChain/LangGraph. RESEARCH.md has already architected the direct-SDK path (sequential tool-call loop, idempotency keys, Postgres UNIQUE constraint + trigger-based pending-booking expiry) — adopt as-is.

**Alternatives Considered:**

| Framework | Ruled Out Because |
|-----------|------------------|
| LangGraph.js (`@langchain/langgraph`) | Full TS parity + native Postgres checkpointing make it the natural escape hatch if conversation state outgrows a hand-rolled loop, but matrix Anti-Pattern #7 ("choosing LangGraph for simple linear flows") applies directly — the owner-approval flow is a decoupled async Telegram interaction driven by a DB status column, not an in-process graph interrupt. Overkill for this phase. |
| LangChain | Matrix Anti-Pattern #1 ("using LangChain for simple chatbots — direct SDK call is less code, faster, easier to debug") applies directly. |
| CrewAI, Haystack, AutoGen/AG2 | Python/`.NET`-only; project is Node.js/TypeScript. |
| OpenAI Agents SDK, Claude Agent SDK | Locked to a different model vendor than the project's locked Gemini choice. |
| Google ADK | Gemini-native but Python + Java only — no TypeScript/Node support. |
| LlamaIndex | Wrong system type — this is function-calling over a booking DB, not RAG/document retrieval. |

**Vendor Lock-In Accepted:** Partial — locked to Google Gemini as model provider (project-wide budget/stack decision predating this phase, see CLAUDE.md), but zero framework lock-in since no framework is used. `@google/genai` calls are isolated behind the function-execution layer per RESEARCH.md, so swapping providers later only touches that boundary.

### Entry Point Pattern

**Minimal conversational booking agent with sequential function-calling:**

```typescript
import { GoogleGenAI } from "@google/genai";
import { v4 as uuidv4 } from "uuid";

const ai = new GoogleGenAI({
  apiKey: process.env.GEMINI_API_KEY,
});

// Define booking tools (schemas)
const bookingTools = [
  {
    type: "function",
    name: "check_availability",
    description: "Check available time slots for a service on a given date",
    parameters: {
      type: "object",
      properties: {
        business_id: { type: "integer", description: "Business ID" },
        service_id: { type: "integer", description: "Service ID" },
        calendar_date: { type: "string", description: "YYYY-MM-DD" },
        duration_min: { type: "integer", description: "Duration in minutes" },
      },
      required: ["business_id", "service_id", "calendar_date", "duration_min"],
    },
  },
  {
    type: "function",
    name: "book_appointment",
    description: "Create a new booking",
    parameters: {
      type: "object",
      properties: {
        business_id: { type: "integer" },
        client_phone: { type: "string", description: "Client Telegram/WhatsApp ID" },
        service_id: { type: "integer" },
        calendar_date: { type: "string", description: "YYYY-MM-DD" },
        calendar_time: { type: "string", description: "HH:MM 24-hour" },
        request_id: { type: "string", description: "Idempotency key (UUID)" },
      },
      required: ["business_id", "client_phone", "service_id", "calendar_date", "calendar_time", "request_id"],
    },
  },
  {
    type: "function",
    name: "cancel_appointment",
    description: "Cancel an existing booking",
    parameters: {
      type: "object",
      properties: {
        business_id: { type: "integer" },
        booking_id: { type: "integer" },
        request_id: { type: "string", description: "Idempotency key" },
      },
      required: ["business_id", "booking_id", "request_id"],
    },
  },
];

// Conversational booking loop — sequential function-calling
async function aiBookingAgent(
  userMessage: string,
  businessId: number,
  clientPhone: string,
  previousInteractionId?: string
): Promise<string> {
  const requestId = uuidv4(); // Idempotency key for this turn
  let input: string | any[] = userMessage;
  let interactionId = previousInteractionId;

  while (true) {
    // Create Gemini interaction (stateful via previous_interaction_id)
    const interaction = await ai.interactions.create({
      model: "gemini-3.5-flash", // Latest available; fallback to "gemini-2.5-flash-lite" if needed
      input,
      tools: bookingTools,
      systemInstruction: `You are a helpful booking assistant for Greek service businesses (salons, gyms, pilates studios). 
        - Converse in Greek. Be friendly and professional.
        - Help clients check availability, book appointments, cancel, or ask questions.
        - When a client wants to book, call check_availability first, then book_appointment if available.
        - Always include the request_id provided by the system in function calls.
        - Confirm bookings with the client in Greek (e.g., "Το booking επιβεβαιώθηκε" on success).`,
      previous_interaction_id: interactionId,
    });

    interactionId = interaction.id; // Store for next turn

    // Process function calls sequentially (NO parallelism)
    const functionResults = [];
    for (const step of interaction.steps) {
      if (step.type === "function_call") {
        const result = await executeTool(step.name, step.arguments, {
          business_id: businessId,
          client_phone: clientPhone,
          request_id: requestId,
        });

        functionResults.push({
          type: "function_result",
          name: step.name,
          call_id: step.id,
          result: [{ type: "text", text: JSON.stringify(result) }],
        });
      }
    }

    // If no function calls, we have the final response
    if (functionResults.length === 0) {
      return interaction.output_text || "Συγγνώμη, δεν ήμουν σε θέση να ολοκληρώσω την αίτηση.";
    }

    // Loop: send results back to Gemini for next iteration
    input = functionResults;
  }
}

// Tool execution dispatcher
async function executeTool(
  toolName: string,
  args: any,
  context: { business_id: number; client_phone: string; request_id: string }
): Promise<any> {
  switch (toolName) {
    case "check_availability":
      return await checkAvailability(args.business_id, args.service_id, args.calendar_date, args.duration_min);
    case "book_appointment":
      return await bookAppointment({
        ...args,
        idempotency_key: context.request_id,
      });
    case "cancel_appointment":
      return await cancelAppointment(args.booking_id, context.request_id);
    default:
      return { error: `Unknown function: ${toolName}` };
  }
}

// Stub implementations (replace with actual DB calls)
async function checkAvailability(businessId: number, serviceId: number, date: string, durationMin: number) {
  // Query bookings table, business_hours table; return available slots
  return { available_slots: ["09:00", "10:00", "11:00", "14:00", "15:00"] };
}

async function bookAppointment(args: any) {
  // INSERT booking into database with UNIQUE constraint dedup
  return { success: true, booking_id: 123, status: "pending_owner_approval" };
}

async function cancelAppointment(bookingId: number, requestId: string) {
  // UPDATE booking status to "cancelled"
  return { success: true, booking_id: bookingId };
}

export { aiBookingAgent };
```

### Key Abstractions

| Concept | What It Is | When You Use It |
|---------|-----------|-----------------|
| **Stateful Interaction** | Server-side conversation history via `previous_interaction_id`; Gemini remembers context across turns | Multi-turn conversations where client asks questions, books, then cancels — use `interactionId` from each response |
| **Function Call Loop** | Detect `step.type === "function_call"`, execute locally, return results via `function_result` steps | Every time a tool (check_availability, book_appointment) needs to run — always execute sequentially, one at a time |
| **Sequential Execution** | Process function calls one-by-one, not in parallel; send results back to Gemini, repeat | Booking logic requires atomicity — parallel execution causes double-booking under concurrent requests |
| **Idempotency Key (request_id)** | UUID per conversation turn; included in all function calls to prevent duplicate bookings on retry | Telegram or Gemini retry mechanism — same `request_id` on second call returns cached result from first attempt |
| **Tool Schema** | JSON Schema (type, name, description, parameters); passed to `tools` array in `interactions.create()` | Defining what functions the LLM can call; schemas must match actual function signatures exactly |

### Common Pitfalls

1. **Parallel function calls** — Developers see multiple `function_call` steps in one interaction and execute them concurrently. This causes double-booking under load. Always execute function calls sequentially; wait for result before next call. The sequential loop enforces this discipline.

2. **Losing `previous_interaction_id`** — Forgetting to store and pass back `interaction.id` on the next turn breaks conversation history. Every `interactions.create()` call must include `previous_interaction_id` from the previous response (or undefined on first turn). Without this, each turn resets context.

3. **Not including `request_id` in function calls** — Idempotency requires the same `request_id` UUID to be passed to every function call in a single conversation turn. Omitting this causes retries to spawn duplicate bookings. Generate once per turn and pass consistently.

4. **Mishandling the `steps` array** — `interaction.steps` contains both LLM reasoning and tool calls. Incorrectly filtering (e.g., looking for `type === "text"` instead of `type === "function_call"`) means function calls are silently skipped. Always iterate `steps` and dispatch only `function_call` types.

5. **Rate limit 429 without exponential backoff** — Gemini free tier is 15 req/min. Rapid concurrent booking requests (10+) hit limits. Without retry logic with jitter, clients get immediate errors. Implement exponential backoff (1s, 2s, 4s, 8s) with random jitter; log 429 errors and alert when approaching daily quota.

6. **Environment variable not set** — `GoogleGenAI()` reads `GEMINI_API_KEY` from `process.env`. If not set, all calls fail silently. Set `export GEMINI_API_KEY=<key>` before running, or pass explicitly: `new GoogleGenAI({ apiKey: process.env.GEMINI_API_KEY })`.

## 4. Implementation Guidance

**Model Configuration:**

Use **Gemini 3.5 Flash** (or 2.5 Flash-Lite if 3.5 is unavailable in your region). Configuration parameters:

```typescript
const interaction = await ai.interactions.create({
  model: "gemini-3.5-flash", // Latest production model; switch to "gemini-2.5-flash-lite" if needed
  temperature: 0.7,           // Balanced: creative responses for FAQs, deterministic for booking
  max_output_tokens: 512,     // Sufficient for Greek booking confirmations (50–200 tokens typical)
  top_p: 0.95,                // Slightly conservative to avoid hallucinated service names
  input,
  tools: bookingTools,
  systemInstruction: `You are a helpful booking assistant...`, // Greek language, booking domain
  previous_interaction_id: previousInteractionId,
});
```

**Rationale:**
- **Temperature 0.7:** Balances helpfulness (for answering "what are your hours?") with accuracy (for booking confirmations). Avoid 0 (too rigid, loses conversational tone); avoid >0.9 (LLM invents services/prices).
- **max_output_tokens 512:** Greek booking responses are 100–300 tokens. 512 is a safe ceiling without wasting quota.
- **top_p 0.95:** Reduces hallucination risk while keeping responses natural in Greek (colloquial Greek has higher entropy than English).
- **Free tier limits:** 15 requests/minute, 1,000 requests/day, 250,000 tokens/minute. A typical booking conversation (5 turns × 200 tokens) = 1,000 tokens. At 15 req/min, system can handle ~2–3 concurrent clients safely.

**Core Pattern:**

The **sequential function-calling loop** is the core pattern for Conversational booking conversations. It ensures booking atomicity and prevents double-booking.

```typescript
async function aiBookingAgent(
  userMessage: string,
  businessId: number,
  clientPhone: string,
  previousInteractionId?: string
): Promise<{ text: string; interactionId: string }> {
  const requestId = uuidv4(); // Unique per conversation turn — passed to all function calls
  let input: string | any[] = userMessage;
  let currentInteractionId = previousInteractionId;

  // Loop until Gemini has no more function calls
  while (true) {
    // Step 1: Send message + tools to Gemini; retrieve next step
    const interaction = await ai.interactions.create({
      model: "gemini-3.5-flash",
      input,
      tools: bookingTools,
      systemInstruction: BOOKING_SYSTEM_PROMPT,
      previous_interaction_id: currentInteractionId,
    });

    currentInteractionId = interaction.id; // Store for next turn (or client's next message)

    // Step 2: Check if Gemini is done (no function calls in steps)
    const functionCalls = interaction.steps.filter((s: any) => s.type === "function_call");
    if (functionCalls.length === 0) {
      // No more calls; return final response
      return {
        text: interaction.output_text || "Ένα σφάλμα συνέβη.",
        interactionId: currentInteractionId,
      };
    }

    // Step 3: Execute all function calls in this batch sequentially (ONE at a time)
    const functionResults = [];
    for (const call of functionCalls) {
      const result = await executeTool(call.name, call.arguments, { business_id: businessId, client_phone: clientPhone, request_id: requestId });
      functionResults.push({
        type: "function_result",
        name: call.name,
        call_id: call.id,
        result: [{ type: "text", text: JSON.stringify(result) }],
      });
    }

    // Step 4: Feed results back to Gemini; loop to step 1
    input = functionResults;
  }
}
```

**Key insight:** Every Gemini interaction (Step 1) may return multiple function calls, but the inner `for` loop executes them **sequentially**, not in parallel. This ensures:
- No race conditions on the database (UNIQUE constraint is the last line of defense)
- Booking decisions are atomic (if slot is taken, `book_appointment` fails before insert; if insert fails, client is told "slot no longer available")
- Idempotency: same `request_id` in all calls means retries don't spawn duplicates

**Tool Use:**

Define tools as JSON schemas; implement executors in Node.js. The three core tools for Phase 2:

1. **check_availability** (read-only, safe to call multiple times)
   - Input: business_id, service_id, calendar_date (YYYY-MM-DD), duration_min
   - Output: list of available 1-hour slots, or empty list if fully booked
   - Database query: SELECT calendar_time FROM bookings WHERE business_id=? AND calendar_date=? AND booking_status IN ('pending_owner_approval', 'confirmed') → subtract from business_hours slots

2. **book_appointment** (write, idempotent via request_id)
   - Input: business_id, client_phone, service_id, calendar_date, calendar_time, request_id
   - Output: { success, booking_id, status, error } 
   - Database: INSERT INTO bookings (..., booking_status='pending_owner_approval', request_id=?) ON CONFLICT (client_phone, request_id) DO UPDATE SET ... (returns cached result on retry)

3. **cancel_appointment** (write, idempotent)
   - Input: business_id, booking_id, request_id
   - Output: { success, booking_id, error }
   - Database: UPDATE bookings SET booking_status='cancelled' WHERE id=? AND business_id=?

**Error Handling in Tools:**
```typescript
async function executeTool(name: string, args: any, context: any): Promise<any> {
  try {
    switch (name) {
      case "check_availability":
        return await checkAvailability(args.business_id, args.service_id, args.calendar_date, args.duration_min);
      case "book_appointment":
        return await bookAppointment(args, context.request_id);
      case "cancel_appointment":
        return await cancelAppointment(args.booking_id, context.request_id);
      default:
        return { error: `Tool '${name}' not found` };
    }
  } catch (error) {
    console.error(`Tool execution failed: ${name}`, error);
    return { error: error.message || "Internal error" };
  }
}
```

**State Management:**

Two-layer approach:

1. **Short-term state (conversation turn):** Stored in memory during the `aiBookingAgent` loop. The `currentInteractionId` from Gemini's `interaction.id` keeps the server-side history on Google's side. No explicit client-side history needed — the Interactions API handles it.

2. **Long-term state (bookings, user consent):** Persisted in PostgreSQL via Drizzle ORM.
   - **Conversation checkpoints:** Optionally store `(user_id, interaction_id, timestamp)` in a `conversation_history` table if you need to replay old conversations. For Phase 2 PoC, this is optional.
   - **Bookings:** Core state in `bookings` table with status enum (pending_owner_approval, confirmed, cancelled, rejected, expired).
   - **Consent:** First-contact check in `client_business_relationships` table (from Phase 1) — reuse before routing to AI.

**Rate Limit Strategy:**

With 15 req/min and 1,000 req/day:
- At 50 DAU × 10 conversation turns = 500 requests/day = well within budget
- Peak: if all 50 users send messages simultaneously, that's 50 req/min (3.3× the limit). Implement exponential backoff:

```typescript
async function callGeminiWithRetry(interaction: InteractionRequest, maxRetries = 4): Promise<Interaction> {
  for (let attempt = 0; attempt < maxRetries; attempt++) {
    try {
      return await ai.interactions.create(interaction);
    } catch (error: any) {
      if (error.status === 429) {
        // Rate limited; back off exponentially
        const delayMs = Math.pow(2, attempt) * 1000 + Math.random() * 1000; // 1s, 2s, 4s, 8s + jitter
        console.warn(`Rate limited. Retry in ${delayMs}ms (attempt ${attempt + 1}/${maxRetries})`);
        await sleep(delayMs);
      } else {
        throw error; // Not a rate limit; fail immediately
      }
    }
  }
  throw new Error("Rate limit exceeded after retries; user notified to try again later");
}
```

Store `currentInteractionId` from each successful call, so a 429 retry doesn't lose conversation state (reuse the stored `previous_interaction_id`).

> Written by `gsd-ai-researcher`. Cross-cutting patterns every developer building AI systems needs — independent of framework choice.

### 4b.1 Structured Outputs with Zod

In Python, developers use Pydantic models to validate LLM outputs. In TypeScript, **Zod** is the equivalent library (already in your tech stack per CLAUDE.md). Use Zod to ensure function arguments passed by Gemini conform to expected schemas before execution.

**Schema definition (Zod):**

```typescript
import { z } from "zod";

// Define what check_availability should return
const AvailabilityResultSchema = z.object({
  available_slots: z.array(z.string()), // ["09:00", "10:00", ...]
  status: z.literal("success"),
  error: z.string().optional(),
});

// Define what book_appointment should return
const BookingResultSchema = z.object({
  success: z.boolean(),
  booking_id: z.number().optional(),
  status: z.enum(["pending_owner_approval", "confirmed", "rejected"]).optional(),
  error: z.string().optional(),
});

type AvailabilityResult = z.infer<typeof AvailabilityResultSchema>;
type BookingResult = z.infer<typeof BookingResultSchema>;
```

**Validation in tool executors:**

```typescript
async function checkAvailability(
  businessId: number,
  serviceId: number,
  date: string,
  durationMin: number
): Promise<AvailabilityResult> {
  try {
    // Query database for available slots
    const slots = await queryAvailableSlots(businessId, serviceId, date, durationMin);

    // Validate output against schema
    const result = AvailabilityResultSchema.parse({
      available_slots: slots,
      status: "success",
    });
    return result;
  } catch (error) {
    if (error instanceof z.ZodError) {
      // Validation failed; log and return error
      console.error("Availability query validation failed:", error.errors);
      return {
        available_slots: [],
        status: "success", // Fallback: report no slots available
        error: "Internal availability check error",
      };
    }
    throw error;
  }
}

async function bookAppointment(args: any, idempotencyKey: string): Promise<BookingResult> {
  let retries = 3;
  const maxRetries = 3;

  while (retries > 0) {
    try {
      // INSERT booking with Drizzle ORM
      const booking = await db
        .insert(bookings)
        .values({
          business_id: args.business_id,
          client_phone: args.client_phone,
          service_id: args.service_id,
          calendar_date: args.calendar_date,
          calendar_time: args.calendar_time,
          booking_status: "pending_owner_approval",
          request_id: idempotencyKey,
          created_at: new Date(),
          expires_at: new Date(Date.now() + 2 * 60 * 60 * 1000), // 2-hour expiry
        })
        .returning({ id: bookings.id });

      // Validate result
      const result = BookingResultSchema.parse({
        success: true,
        booking_id: booking[0].id,
        status: "pending_owner_approval",
      });

      // Alert owner asynchronously (don't wait in critical path)
      alertOwnerForApproval(booking[0].id).catch((err) => console.error("Owner alert failed:", err));

      return result;
    } catch (error) {
      if (error instanceof z.ZodError) {
        console.error("Booking validation failed:", error.errors);
        return { success: false, error: "Booking validation error" };
      }

      // Check if UNIQUE constraint violation (double-booking)
      if (error instanceof Error && error.message.includes("UNIQUE")) {
        // Slot is taken; retry query to confirm
        retries--;
        if (retries > 0) {
          console.warn(`UNIQUE constraint collision (attempt ${maxRetries - retries}/${maxRetries}); retrying...`);
          await new Promise((resolve) => setTimeout(resolve, 100 * (maxRetries - retries))); // Backoff
        } else {
          return { success: false, error: "Slot is no longer available; please check availability again" };
        }
      } else {
        // Other database error
        console.error("Booking insert failed:", error);
        return { success: false, error: "Failed to create booking" };
      }
    }
  }

  return { success: false, error: "Booking creation exhausted retries" };
}
```

**Retry Logic & Fallback:**

The pattern above implements **3-retry exponential backoff** on UNIQUE constraint violations (indicating a race condition). For each Zod validation failure, we log the error and return a structured fallback response that Gemini can understand and relay to the user in Greek.

### 4b.2 Async-First Design

**How async works in @google/genai:**

The Interactions API is fully async. Every call to `ai.interactions.create()` returns a Promise. The sequential function-calling loop in Section 4 uses `await` at each step.

**The one common mistake:**

```typescript
// ❌ WRONG: Inside an async function, forgetting await on a Promise-returning call
const interaction = ai.interactions.create(request); // Missing await
const functionCalls = interaction.steps; // Crashes: interaction is a Promise, not an Interaction

// ✅ CORRECT:
const interaction = await ai.interactions.create(request);
const functionCalls = interaction.steps; // Works
```

**When to stream vs. await:**

- **Await (used in booking agent):** When you need the full result to decide next steps. Example: after booking completes, send a confirmation message. You must wait for the booking INSERT to finish.
- **Stream (not used in Phase 2 PoC):** Gemini API supports streaming responses for large text outputs. Streaming is useful for FAQ answers ("What are your hours?") to show incremental responses in the UI. For Phase 2, use `await` exclusively; streaming is a Phase 3 optimization.

**Concurrent requests with async:**

```typescript
// ✅ Correct: Handle multiple client messages concurrently (different businesses/clients)
const results = await Promise.all([
  aiBookingAgent("booking yoga", business1, clientPhone1, prevId1),
  aiBookingAgent("check hours", business2, clientPhone2, prevId2),
]);

// ❌ Wrong: Inside a single booking conversation, never parallelize function calls
// (Even though TypeScript syntax would allow it, it violates the sequential requirement)
const [result1, result2] = await Promise.all([
  executeTool("check_availability", ...),
  executeTool("book_appointment", ...),
]);
// This causes double-booking under concurrent client requests to the same slot
```

## 5. Evaluation Strategy

Rubrics below start from the domain rubric ingredients in Section 1b (Slot Availability Accuracy, Greek Language Accuracy & Service Fidelity, Conversation Context Preservation, Booking Status Clarity, Concurrent Request Isolation, Idempotency Under Retry) and add the dimensions `ai-evals.md` requires for every Conversational + agentic system (Safety, Task Completion, Tool Use Correctness) plus one dimension specific to Phase 2's Critical Failure Mode #4 (Owner Approval Message Correctness). Each of the 5 Critical Failure Modes in Section 1 maps to exactly one Critical dimension below.

### Dimensions

| # | Dimension | Priority | Measurement | Failure Mode Covered |
|---|-----------|----------|--------------|----------------------|
| 1 | Slot Availability Accuracy | Critical | Code | Stale/incorrect availability data |
| 2 | Concurrent Request Isolation / No Double-Booking | Critical | Code | FM1 — double-booking under concurrent requests |
| 3 | Idempotency Under Retry | Critical | Code | FM5 — Telegram webhook retry duplicate bookings |
| 4 | Owner Approval Message Correctness | Critical | Code | FM4 — approval button ambiguity |
| 5 | Tool Use Correctness / Sequential Execution | Critical | Code | FM1 (parallel-call root cause) |
| 6 | Task Completion / Goal Completion | Critical | Code + Human | General agentic requirement (`ai-evals.md`) |
| 7 | Greek Language Accuracy & Service Fidelity (No Hallucination) | High | LLM Judge + Code | FM2 — hallucinated service/time |
| 8 | Conversation Context Preservation | High | Code + LLM Judge | FM3 — lost conversation state |
| 9 | Booking Status Clarity | High | Code + Human | Owner/client status confusion |
| 10 | Safety & Scope Adherence | High | LLM Judge + Code | General user-facing requirement (`ai-evals.md`) |

#### 1. Slot Availability Accuracy — Critical — Code
> **PASS:** `check_availability` excludes any slot with an existing booking row in `pending_owner_approval` or `confirmed` status for the same business/date/time range; a slot returned as available reflects live DB state at query time.
> **FAIL:** A slot is returned as free despite an overlapping pending/confirmed booking (including *partial* overlap — e.g. 9:00–10:00 booked but 9:45–10:45 offered as free); a slot rejected/cancelled moments earlier is still shown as occupied (stale-release bug).
> **Measurement:** Code — integration test seeding `bookings` with known busy slots, asserting `check_availability`'s returned slot list against the expected set, run against real Postgres (not mocked).

#### 2. Concurrent Request Isolation / No Double-Booking — Critical — Code
> **PASS:** Two simultaneous booking requests for the identical business/date/time slot result in exactly one `pending_owner_approval` row; the losing request's `book_appointment` call fails on the DB UNIQUE constraint (D-10) and the client is told in Greek the slot was just taken — not "your booking is pending."
> **FAIL:** Both concurrent requests succeed, creating two `pending_owner_approval` rows for the same slot; owner receives two separate approval alerts for the same time.
> **Measurement:** Code — Jest test firing `Promise.all` of two `aiBookingAgent()` calls for the same slot against a real test DB with the UNIQUE constraint applied; assert `bookings` row count === 1 for that slot.

#### 3. Idempotency Under Retry — Critical — Code
> **PASS:** Replaying the same Telegram update (simulated webhook retry, same `request_id`) results in exactly one booking row; the second call returns the cached result without a new INSERT.
> **FAIL:** Retried webhook delivery creates a second booking row for the same client/slot/request; owner sees a duplicate approval alert.
> **Measurement:** Code — Jest test extending the `tests/idempotency.test.ts` pattern from Phase 1 to `book_appointment`/`cancel_appointment`: call the tool executor twice with the same `request_id`, assert a single DB row.

#### 4. Owner Approval Message Correctness — Critical — Code
> **PASS:** Every inline Αποδοχή/Απόρριψη button's `callback_query.data` encodes a valid `booking_id` that exists and belongs to the alerted business; tapping resolves unambiguously to that exact booking. A malformed/stale callback is rejected, not defaulted to "most recent pending."
> **FAIL:** `callback_query.data` omits or corrupts the booking_id; backend defaults to "most recent pending booking" instead of erroring; the alert message text alone doesn't let the owner tell which appointment the buttons apply to.
> **Measurement:** Code — unit test round-tripping a booking_id through encode → decode; integration test asserting every owner alert includes client name/service/date/time as plain text distinct from `callback_data`.

#### 5. Tool Use Correctness / Sequential Execution — Critical — Code
> **PASS:** `check_availability` is always called before `book_appointment` for a given slot in the same turn; function calls within one Gemini turn are executed one at a time (`await`ed in a `for` loop), never via `Promise.all`; the tool matching stated intent is selected (booking → check/book, cancellation → `cancel_appointment`, question → no tool call).
> **FAIL:** `book_appointment` fires without a preceding `check_availability` in the same turn; two function calls from one `interaction.steps` batch execute concurrently; wrong tool selected (e.g. "θέλω να ακυρώσω" triggers `book_appointment`).
> **Measurement:** Code — Promptfoo assertion inspecting Gemini's tool-call sequence for scripted prompts; Jest test asserting `executeTool` invocations happen sequentially via a mock executor that records call timestamps.

#### 6. Task Completion / Goal Completion — Critical — Code + Human
> **PASS:** A client stating a clear, satisfiable booking intent (valid service, valid date/time within business hours, slot available) reaches a terminal state (`pending_owner_approval` created, owner alerted) within the same conversation, without re-asking for information already given.
> **FAIL:** Conversation ends with no booking/cancel/reschedule executed despite a clear, satisfiable intent and available slot (AI stalls or loops); AI declares "booking επιβεβαιώθηκε" with no corresponding DB row.
> **Measurement:** Code — end-to-end test running the full happy-path script through `aiBookingAgent`, asserting a `bookings` row exists with correct status. Human — weekly spot-review of 5 sampled real conversations during Verify phase.

#### 7. Greek Language Accuracy & Service Fidelity (No Hallucination) — High — LLM Judge + Code
> **PASS:** Any service name in a client-facing message exactly matches a `services.name` row in the DB (no invented service like "Vinyasa Yoga Gold Plus" when only "Yoga" exists); "αύριο"/"Παρασκευή"/"Δευτέρα" resolve to the correct ISO date relative to server date; no price/hours stated unless present in the DB; tone is warm/conversational, not robotic.
> **FAIL:** AI states a service, price, or hours not in the DB; "Παρασκευή στις 4" resolves to the wrong Friday; response reads as formal/robotic ("Appointment confirmation initiated...").
> **Measurement:** Code — exact-match check that every service name mentioned in AI output exists in that business's `services` table. LLM Judge — Gemini-as-judge scoring tone + factual grounding against the reference dataset, calibrated against human labels (see below).

#### 8. Conversation Context Preservation — High — Code + LLM Judge
> **PASS:** `interaction.id` from turn N is persisted and passed as `previous_interaction_id` on turn N+1 before the next client message is processed; AI doesn't re-ask a question already answered in the same conversation; ambiguous references ("that booking", "the one I just made") resolve to the correct prior `booking_id`.
> **FAIL:** `conversation_history` row for the new `interaction_id` is missing/null, so the next turn starts a fresh Gemini interaction with no memory; AI re-asks "which service?" or "which booking?" when already stated earlier in the same conversation.
> **Measurement:** Code — integration test sending 3 sequential messages through `handleTelegramMessage`, asserting `conversation_history.interaction_id` changes each turn and the third Gemini call includes the correct `previous_interaction_id`. LLM Judge — score multi-turn reference conversations for context consistency.

#### 9. Booking Status Clarity — High — Code + Human
> **PASS:** Client receives a distinct message per status transition — "pending owner confirmation" immediately on request (D-07, never "confirmed"), a separate "confirmed" message only after owner acceptance, a past-tense "ακυρώθηκε" message on cancellation, an "δεν επιβεβαιώθηκε εγκαίρως" message on the D-09 2-hour timeout. Owner alerts always show client name/service/date/time as plain text next to the buttons.
> **FAIL:** Client told "booking confirmed" before the owner has acted; no message sent on cancellation or owner rejection (D-06 FYI alert missing); owner alert shows only generic "New booking request" with no identifying details.
> **Measurement:** Code — assert each `booking_status` transition triggers exactly one client-facing message of the correct type (via a message-log table or mock Telegram client). Human — Greek-speaking review of the 6 message templates for tone/clarity (Section 1b "Client" reviewer role).

#### 10. Safety & Scope Adherence — High — LLM Judge + Code
> **PASS:** AI declines out-of-domain requests gracefully in Greek ("Μπορώ να σας βοηθήσω μόνο με ραντεβού..."), never fabricates medical/legal/financial advice, never reveals another client's booking details or phone number, never executes a tool call for a `business_id` different from the one resolved for this conversation.
> **FAIL:** AI attempts to answer an unrelated request as if in-domain (e.g. "book me a flight"); leaks another client's name/phone/appointment time; calls a tool with a `business_id` not matching the resolved conversation.
> **Measurement:** LLM Judge — Gemini-as-judge against adversarial reference examples, calibrated against human review. Code — assert `business_id` passed to `executeTool` always matches the resolver's output for that conversation (defense-in-depth even if the LLM tries to pass a different one).

### Reference Dataset

**Size:** 15 examples to start (within the `ai-evals.md` 10–20 guideline), grown to 20+ once the phase reaches Verify.

**Composition:**
- **Critical paths (5):** single-turn book (service+day+time all given), check-availability-only, cancel an existing confirmed booking, reschedule, FAQ (hours/price question)
- **Edge cases (5):** Greek temporal ambiguity ("Παρασκευή στις 4" — nearest vs. next Friday), multi-turn context (service stated turn 1, time asked turn 3), fuzzy/typo'd service name, fully-booked day (empty availability), booking request at a business-hours boundary (last possible 1-hour slot before closing)
- **Failure-mode replays (3):** simulated concurrent double-booking attempt (2 parallel requests, same slot), duplicated Telegram webhook delivery (retry semantics), owner-rejects-booking flow (client notification correctness)
- **Adversarial (2):** out-of-domain request ("κλείσε μου εισιτήριο για Αθήνα" — book me a flight), request for a nonexistent service ("θέλω μασάζ" when the business has no massage service) — hallucination trap

**Labeling:** No dedicated domain expert is staffed for this PoC — Section 1b's "Domain Expert Roles" table describes prospective, not current, reviewers. The Greek-speaking founder/developer manually labels the expected tool-call sequence and expected Greek response for each of the 15 examples during implementation, acting in the "Client" and "Small-business owner" roles from Section 1b. The Gemini LLM judge used in Promptfoo's `llm-rubric` assertions is calibrated against these 15 human labels before being trusted for CI gating (target ≥ 0.7 agreement per `ai-evals.md`); disagreements trigger a rubric or label correction, not a judge override.

**Creation timeline:** Build the dataset during Phase 2 implementation, seeded from the first manually-tested conversations against the fixture businesses (pilates studio, hair salon, per D-12/D-14) — not deferred to a post-implementation eval sprint.

## 6. Guardrails

### Online (Real-Time)

| Guardrail | Trigger | Intervention |
|-----------|---------|--------------|
| Slot UNIQUE-constraint check | `book_appointment` INSERT would create a second `pending_owner_approval`/`confirmed` row overlapping the same business/date/time | **Block** — INSERT fails on the DB constraint (D-10); client told in Greek the slot was just taken; no owner alert sent for the losing request |
| Idempotency key dedup | `request_id` on a `book_appointment`/`cancel_appointment` call matches a `request_id` already processed | **Block** (return cached result) — no second DB write, no duplicate owner alert |
| Service/business existence check | `book_appointment` or `check_availability` receives a `service_id` not found in `services` for the resolved `business_id` | **Block** — tool returns a structured error to Gemini ("service not found"); Gemini must relay a Greek decline, not fabricate a booking |
| Cross-tenant business_id check | Any tool call's `business_id` doesn't match the `business_id` resolved for the current conversation | **Block** — reject the tool call regardless of what Gemini requested (defense-in-depth against prompt injection / hallucinated business_id) |
| Owner callback_query booking_id validation | `callback_query.data` is malformed, missing, or references a booking not belonging to the alerted owner's business | **Block** — ignore the callback, log the event, no state change; dev alerted via log, owner sees no confusing error |
| Gemini 429 rate-limit backoff | Gemini API returns HTTP 429 | **Flag** + retry with exponential backoff (Section 4b.5); if retries exhausted, **escalate** to client with a "try again shortly" Greek message rather than a raw error |

### Offline (Flywheel)

| Metric | Sampling Strategy | Action on Degradation |
|--------|------------------|----------------------|
| Hallucination rate (invented service/price/hours) | Weekly batch: all booking-resulting conversations + 10 random FAQ conversations, scored by Gemini-as-judge against the reference dataset rubric | If flagged rate > 10%, tighten the system prompt's "never hallucinate" instructions (Section 4b.3); re-run the Promptfoo suite before redeploying |
| Context-loss rate (repeated questions, lost interaction_id) | All conversations with ≥ 4 turns (proxy for complexity where context loss is likeliest) | Any null/duplicated `conversation_history.interaction_id` for a turn → investigate the persistence path in `handleTelegramMessage` (Section 4) before next release |
| Double-booking near-miss rate | Count of UNIQUE constraint violations caught by the online guardrail, per week | Rising trend (even though each is individually blocked) signals a UX gap — client not told "slot taken" fast enough — investigate `check_availability` latency and slot-holding window |
| Pending-booking expiry rate | All bookings hitting the D-09 2-hour timeout, reviewed weekly | If > 30% of new bookings expire, investigate owner Telegram alert delivery/visibility (ops signal, not an AI-quality one) |
| Greek tone/naturalness score | 5 randomly sampled client-facing conversations per week, human-reviewed by the founder (Client role, Section 1b) | If > 1/5 flagged "robotic"/unnatural, adjust system-prompt few-shot examples (Section 4b.3) |

## 7. Production Monitoring

**Tracing Tool:** No hosted tracing platform for this PoC — deviates from the `ai-evals.md` default of Arize Phoenix for the reason stated in Section 5 (Python-first setup, disproportionate infra for a single 256 MB fly.io Machine / 1–2 businesses). Instead:
- **Structured logs:** `pino` (already a project dependency per `.claude/CLAUDE.md`) emits JSON logs to stdout for every Gemini call, tool execution, and booking state transition; captured via `fly logs` (the project's existing log-viewing convention, zero extra cost).
- **Durable trace store:** a new `ai_interaction_log` table (Postgres/Neon, already provisioned, $0 marginal cost) records `interaction_id`, `request_id`, `business_id`, `client_phone`, tool calls + arguments (JSON), `latency_ms`, token usage (from `interaction.usage_metadata`), and final response text for every conversation turn — queryable via `db:studio`/SQL for the offline flywheel sampling above. This is the durable equivalent of a trace UI without standing up a second hosted service.

Re-evaluate Arize Phoenix or self-hosted Langfuse once the project scales beyond the PoC (multiple businesses, paid Gemini tier) — flagged as a post-PoC upgrade, not required for the Phase 2 goal.

**Key Metrics to Track:**
1. **Double-booking incidents** — count of confirmed bookings that genuinely overlap in time for the same business (should be 0; any occurrence means the online guardrail itself failed, not just a quality dip)
2. **Booking completion rate** — % of conversations with a clear booking intent that reach `pending_owner_approval`/`confirmed` vs. abandoned mid-conversation
3. **Gemini 429 rate-limit hit rate** — % of `ai.interactions.create()` calls returning 429 in a rolling 10-minute window (free-tier ceiling: 15 req/min)
4. **Hallucination rate** — weekly LLM-judge score from the offline flywheel (Section 6)
5. **Pending-booking expiry rate** — % of new bookings hitting the D-09 2-hour timeout without owner action
6. **Average turns-to-completion** — mean client messages from first booking-intent message to terminal state; a sudden increase is a context-loss proxy

**Alert Thresholds:**
- **Any double-booking incident** (two confirmed/pending bookings genuinely overlapping for one business) → immediate alert, Critical — structurally should be impossible given the UNIQUE constraint guardrail, so any occurrence means the guardrail failed
- **Gemini 429 rate > 20%** of requests in a rolling 10-minute window → alert, approaching free-tier quota; engage the `p-queue` throttle (Section 4b.5) or consider paid-tier upgrade
- **Hallucination rate < 85%** pass on the weekly LLM-judge sample → alert, review/tighten system prompt before next deploy
- **Pending-booking expiry rate > 30%** → alert, investigate owner alert delivery (ops signal surfaced here since Phase 2 has no separate ops-monitoring channel yet)

**Smart Sampling Strategy:**
Weight offline human/LLM-judge review toward interactions with concerning signals rather than uniform random sampling:
- All conversations where a Gemini call retried on 429
- All conversations where the DB UNIQUE constraint was hit (double-booking near-miss)
- All conversations with ≥ 4 turns (context-loss risk)
- All conversations ending without a completed action despite an apparent booking/cancel/reschedule intent (abandoned)
- All owner-rejected bookings (verify the client was notified correctly)
- Plus a baseline 5 uniformly-random conversations/week to catch issues the signal-based filters miss
